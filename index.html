What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Balance and Control: Learning to balance is key. Start by practicing in a safe, open area, and focus on finding your balance while coasting. If you’re having trouble, try looking forward instead of down, which helps stabilize you. Braking: Mastering the brakes is vital for safety. Most bikes have two brakes—one for the front wheel and one for the rear. Practice using both gently to stop smoothly. The front brake is more powerful, so don’t slam it to avoid flipping over. Pedaling: Get used to the motion of pedaling. Make sure your pedals are at a comfortable position before you start, and keep a consistent rhythm when you pedal.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Age of the Helmet: Most helmets have a shelf life of about 3-5 years from the date of manufacture, even if they haven't been in an accident. The materials in the helmet degrade over time due to exposure to UV light, sweat, and general wear. Check the inside of the helmet for the manufacturing date, often on a sticker inside. Signs of Physical Damage: If your helmet has been involved in a crash or hit hard, it’s crucial to replace it, even if there are no visible cracks. The foam inside may be compressed, which means it won’t protect you as well in the future. Look for any cracks, dents, or scuffs on the outer shell, as well.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Functionality: Squeeze the brake levers to ensure both the front and rear brakes engage properly. If they feel loose or unresponsive, adjust or replace the brake pads. Brake Pads: Check the thickness of the brake pads. If they’re too worn down, you’ll need to replace them for optimal braking performance.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding a bike, always follow traffic rules. Ride on the right side of the road, signal your turns, and obey traffic lights and stop signs. Wear a helmet for safety and use lights or reflectors when riding at night. Stay aware of pedestrians and other cyclists, yielding to them when necessary. Avoid riding on sidewalks unless permitted. Keep a safe distance from vehicles, and be cautious around intersections. Always check your bike’s condition before heading out, and use both brakes gently. Lastly, stay visible and be courteous to others on the road. Safety first!
